Osintly uses a credit-based model. Each search consumes 1 credit. Credits are a standalone currency that can be purchased either as part of a subscription plan or as a one‑off (standalone) purchase.

<Warning>
	Spending credits (for example, running searches or monitor executions) requires an active subscription on the account. You may buy credits without an active subscription, but they cannot be spent until an active subscription is present.
</Warning>

For more details, see [Credits explained](/guides/credits-explained).

## Credit expiry

**Purchased credits** (bought directly) do not expire.

**Promotional or free credits** may have an expiration date as specified in the promotion terms. See [osint.ly/terms](https://rewrite.osint.ly/terms#credits) for details.

---

## What costs a credit

- Any new search query (1 credit per query)
- Re-running an existing search to get fresh results (1 credit)
- Each monitor execution (1 credit per run)

## What does not cost a credit

- Re-opening a past result from history
- Exporting from an existing result
- AI Analyst conversations on a loaded result

---

## Checking your balance

Your balance is visible in three places:

- **Sidebar** - below your username in the left navigation panel
- **Search page** - displayed before running a query
- **Settings > Credits** - full transaction history including search deductions and monitor executions

---

## Managing your subscription

From **Settings > Billing** you can:
- Upgrade or downgrade your plan
- Cancel your subscription (stays active until the end of the current billing period)
- Review past invoices

<Note>All sales are final except where required by applicable law. See [osint.ly/terms](https://rewrite.osint.ly/terms) for the full billing policy.</Note>

---

## Running out of credits

If your balance reaches 0, new searches are blocked. Monitor executions fail until credits are restored. Your history and existing results remain fully accessible.

For a full breakdown of the credit system, see [Credits explained](/guides/credits-explained).
